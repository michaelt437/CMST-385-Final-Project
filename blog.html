6.30.14 - NEXT PROJECT!

So I've been thinking about the next project I should work on, since it has been a while. I want to continue doing movie screen cap paintings. That Ma-Ma painting really turned out better than I expected (people and skin tones are so hard!) so I want to keep the ball rolling. I have collected several screen caps from other movies, including Skyfall, Man of Steel, Harry Potter, and Ironman 3. I found a great site dedicated to having tons and tons of screens from all kinds of movies at movie-screencaps.com. They practically have entire movies in screen cap format!

Looking through the ones I have now, I am leaning towards these two the most. At least there are no faces, but hands are hard too!

6.1.14 - New additions to my wall!


Finally got these guys up on my wall. I have a few more paintings planned for this set. I'm thinking of making them larger than these guys. They will be more armors, but unlike the one I have up now, they will be full body sets. I wish I had thought about this before,
this allows me to practice this new style that I've been trying out. By far these are the most satisfying results to date!

5.19.14 - Almost done!!

Update on Ma-Ma! For those who are new, I started painting this screen shot from the movie Dredd of Ma-Ma. When I watched the movie, this shot immediately jump started my artistic drive and I wanted to try something different. I was always intimidated with the human figure. I've tried references before, but would always quit.

This time, I went straight into the painting. No sketching. It's a method that simulates traditional painting, and it also allows me to practice with color and form rather than lines. My sisters say they can't tell the difference between the screen cap and the painting from afar. Yay!
